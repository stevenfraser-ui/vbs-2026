# Overview

Mission Impossible themed Vacation Bible School (VBS) focusing on how to live the impossible Christian life. Impossible, that is, without Jesus.

### CHARACTERS

- Ethan: Team leader; funny but more serious
- Benjy: Tech guy; funniest
- Ilsa: Smart and arrogant
- Jeremy: Goofy, lovable

## Night 1: Obedience

### Act 1

#### SETTING: IMF Safehouse

- Table
- 2 chairs
- Crates labeled “IMF”
- Techy stuff - boxes, laptops

BENJY is sitting alone in the room, clacking away at a laptop and having a conversation on an ear piece where we can only hear his side. That is until ETHAN and ILSA enter the room. Ilsa is fired up. We can now hear the other side of the conversation. Ilsa and Benjy are arguing about something that went wrong in the mission. Ethan eventually breaks up the argument.
JEREMY shows up late. He’s the new guy. Benjy introduces him to Ethan and Ilsa and provides some background on him. Special forces, highly decorated, yada yada.
Ethan: Benjy, have you had time to analyze the data?
Benjy: Yes, and HQ has already sent us our next mission. [pointing to a tablet on the table]
Ethan picks up the tablet.
“Facial recognition complete. Good morning, Ethan.” - the mission briefing plays.
The debrief plays on the screen (mirroring what Ethan is watching on the tablet).
Thanks to the intel gathered at their last mission, they now know that someone called The Architect created a device called “The Light” and there are multiple interested parties searching for it but no one actually knows what it is.
Some say fountain of youth 
Some say super soldier serum
Some say infinite riches
Debrief will have lots of comments about the importance of following instructions, direction and keeping to the plan.

### Bible Story

Focus on obedience and why it sometimes feels impossible.

### Activities

Gathering intel on The Light
- Who has it? - No idea
- What is it? - software
- Where is it? - a server vault
- When should we go after it?
- Why will it be difficult?
- How do we get it? - 

### Act 2

#### SETTING: Server Vault

Jeremy is the only one on the stage.
Benjy is on a computer somewhere else in the building. 
Ilsa is playing lookout in the lobby.
Ethan is someplace dark with a tablet (maybe only visible on the TV?).
We should either have everyone in different places in the room or have them appear on the screen when they speak. Maybe a mixture of both.

During the mission to retrieve The Light from a secure server vault, Jeremy must cross a pressure-sensitive floor grid while Ethan guides him step-by-step using the correct path. Halfway through, the vault’s security pattern begins changing faster than expected, and Benjy struggles to delay the system from his remote terminal. Believing they are running out of time, Jeremy decides to improvise and jumps directly to the final square instead of waiting for Ethan’s instructions. At first it appears to work, and he begins downloading The Light, but Benjy soon discovers the floor alarm had silently triggered. The vault’s security protocol activates and automatically transfers The Light to another server, causing the mission to fail. The team retreats, and Ethan explains that even a smart decision can lead to failure when instructions and protocol are ignored.

## Night 2: Forgiveness

### Act 1

The agents are again in the safehouse. They briefly discuss the failure of the previous mission and begin discussing plans to try again to recover the Light. All the while, Benjy and Ilsa argue about different ways to go about things. While they review some of their intel on the tablet, their system is hacked. Another player reveals himself as an antagonist who is also after The Light, but seems to have a personal vendetta against this team.
Benjy and Ilsa immediately go to work to trace the hack but they are getting in each other’s way and bickering the whole time, although they do make progress together. When the message is over, Ethan asks if they were able to trace where it came from. Benjy gives some techy speech about why they couldn’t and Ilsa confirms, “it’s just Null.”

### Bible Story

Focus on forgiveness and why it is impossible (without God).

### Activities

Maybe some planning of the mission.
The first part of the mission: obstacle course, duct maze, laser hallway

### Act 2

The end of the mission:
A lot of things have gone wrong on this mission already. We either need to show them somehow or mention them in dialogue or have a pre-recorded scene. 
- Jeremy didn’t make it one of his checkpoints.
- Ilsa faced a challenge and chose to improvise something selfish and arrogant that Benjy didn’t agree with.

Ethan arrives at the door to the computer room. He was supposed to have a copied handprint to open the door but Ilsa’s improvisation broke that part of the plan. Benjy had another idea to send him the handprint on his phone and then also hack the computer to make it think it was a real handprint. Ilsa is working on getting the handprint while Benjy is working to hack the computer the whole time they bicker, delaying the operation. 
Meanwhile, Jeremy is in and out of comms, providing updates on where he is. But mid sentence, he is interrupted, there are fighting noises and then he is gone. No one can reach him.
When he approaches the door, the door is against the back of the stage, but as he gets closer, the door begins to pivot into the middle to reveal to the audience what is on the other side of the door. Ethan stands at the door and communicates with Ilsa and Benjy via comms to try to get the door open.
While Ethan stands on one side of the door, the audience sees on the other side of the door, someone dressed completely in black begins lowering from the ceiling. He reaches the computer, plugs in a device and begins typing. (The screen on the wall shows what is on the computer. He is downloading files.)
Ethan continues to try to gain access. Finally, Ilsa sends the handprint.
Ethan: “Great Ilsa! Now go find Jeremy!”
Ilsa: “On it.”
The handprint doesn’t work at first. 
Ethan: “Benjy, it’s not working.”
Benjy: “Ok, hold on hold on! Ok try it now!”
Ethan: “It worked. There’s a password too.”
Benjy: “Intel didn’t say anything about a password! Ok hang on. I’m already in this shouldn’t be too difficult.”
By now, the other guy has retrieved what he was after and is on his way out.
Benjy: “Ok, the password is 7x77”
Ethan types it in and opens the door. He runs to the computer and begins typing. Only to find that someone had just accessed the files seconds before and then deleted them. He digs a little more and sees the attacker left their signature. “The Null.” He looks up and sees the open ceiling. (It would be cool if he could climb on the desk, jump up and climb into the rafters to chase the guy.)
The team regroups. Ilsa never found Jeremy. Benjy and Ilsa both know they messed up. This is the second time they have lost the Light and this time was directly because of their fighting. And now one of their own is missing - captured or maybe even killed. They apologize and forgive each other - knowing they have to work better together if they are going to accomplish this mission.

## Night 3: Love Your Enemies

### Act 1

Back in the safehouse, the team is back to square 1. Now someone else has the Light. The mood from the end of Night 2 carrries over as this is only moments later. 

They discuss options. Benjy admits this hacker is good. Ethan mentions he must be physical and smart too because he was able to slip into and out of that room. Mid discussion, Ethan notices a tablet on the table. Same as the one they received the mission on. He asks Benjy about it. Benjy isn't sure. They assume it's another message from HQ. He turns it on. "Facial recognition complete. Hello Ethan." 

Except this time, the message is directly from the Null and he delivers a mission of his own for the IMF team. Even though he has the Light, he is unable to decrypt it because it requires a code. He "recruits" Ethan to get the code for him. "Your mission, whether you choose to accept it or not, is to recover my code."
The Null also reveals he has Jeremy in custody and if they don’t do what he asks, he will (kill ?) Jeremy. (Killing is a question because that might be too rough for the kids. Need to think of a stake that is significant without being too violent.)
Unlike before, this isn't a conversation with the team, just a message.

Benjy and Ilsa should voice questions like, "How do we know he's not lying?" and concern about what he will do with the Light if he is able to decrypt it.

Note that the concept of encryption and decryption is way over the heads of young kids so try to find ways to subtly explain that he is unable to access it without a code to explain the concept.

### Bible Story

Focus on loving your enemies. How do you love someone who is threatening to kill someone you care about?

### Activities

Figure out how to decrypt the Light.
Another obstacle course with different things.

### Act 2

**SETTING: The Decryption Core**
Ethan needs to cross a hallway or room that is completely filled with motion-tracking lasers to reach the Decryption Core. The only way to get through it is to have Benjy and Ilsa (who are in separate wings or on comms) simultaneously disable different security modules in exact sequence. They have to pass control back and forth flawlessly to disable sections of the grid as Ethan moves. This serves as a direct callback to Night 1 where Jeremy tried to improvise on the floor grid and ruined the mission. This time, Ethan, Ilsa, and Benjy follow the plan to the exact second, showing true obedience and teamwork.

Once Ethan successfully crosses the grid and reaches the central terminal to retrieve the decryption code. Benjy and Ilsa are in separate wings of the facility handling security bypasses. 
Over comms, Benjy and Ilsa suddenly panic. They report that the facility's security protocols have trapped them (blast doors locking, alarms blaring). The Null has hacked the system. Their comm feeds go dark.
The lights go dark in Ethan's room. The Null walks in. He claims Benjy and Ilsa are trapped and will be eliminated unless Ethan hands over the decryption code. Ethan pulls a drive from the console and hands it over.
Ethan (handing it to him): “This isn’t what you think it is… Jeremy.”
The Null takes off his mask, revealing that he is actually Jeremy.
Ethan explains that he knew it was Jeremy ever since the mysterious tablet appeared in their secure safehouse. Instead of showing anger, Ethan offers Jeremy forgiveness. He explains the lesson: God commands us to love our enemies and offers us grace, and Ethan is offering that same grace to Jeremy. He begs Jeremy to make it right.
Jeremy scoffs, rejecting the forgiveness because of his pride. He refuses to follow their rules, claims he doesn't need them, and tries to escape with the code.
However, as Jeremy runs out, he immediately runs into a perfectly fine Benjy and Ilsa who apprehend him.
Ethan explains they faked the comms blackout. Because Ethan realized Jeremy's identity earlier, the perfectly united IMF team laid a trap for him to subvert his double-cross.
Ethan reveals the drive he gave Jeremy was a fake. He steps back to the terminal and types in the real decryption code: the Bible verse from the lesson. 
The Light is successfully decrypted. Ethan explains that The Light isn't a weapon or money—it is the Word of God; instructions for how to depend on God and live the impossible life.
