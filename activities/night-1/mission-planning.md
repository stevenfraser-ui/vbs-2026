# Mission Planning

## Setup
- **Materials Required:**
  - 1 large bulletin board, magnetic whiteboard, or wall space ("The Assignment Board")
  - 8 "Agent Dossier" cards, each with a character portrait, name, and 2-3 specific skills/traits.
  - 5 "Mission Debrief" cards representing the missions.
- **Preparation:** Set up the board with the 8 tasks pinned in a row. Shuffle the Agent Dossier cards and place them on a table nearby.

## The Agents
These are the 8 field agents available for the mission:
1. **Agent Avery:** Expert rock climber, very strong. *Weakness:* Afraid of the dark.
2. **Agent Blake:** Master hacker and computer genius. *Weakness:* Cannot run or do physical tasks.
3. **Agent Casey:** Disguise expert, speaks 5 languages (including French). *Weakness:* Allergic to dogs.
4. **Agent Drew:** Demolitions and heavy gadgets expert. *Weakness:* Always carries a heavy, noisy 50lb backpack.
5. **Agent Eden:** Olympic gymnast and contortionist. *Weakness:* Cannot read maps.
6. **Agent Finn:** Night-vision and stealth specialist. *Weakness:* Terrible with technology.
7. **Agent Gwen:** Communications expert and tactician. *Weakness:* Heights make her dizzy.
8. **Agent Hunter:** Professional animal wrangler and K9 unit handler. *Weakness:* Clumsy stealth.

## Mission Briefings
The kids will be presented with a full Mission Briefing. They must listen to the parameters, discuss, and select a team of 3-4 agents whose skills cover all the mission requirements without their weaknesses jeopardizing the operation.

### Mission Briefing 1: Operation Blackout
**The Scenario:** "Recruits, a rival agency has stolen our master blueprint and hidden it in an underground bunker. The power to the bunker has been completely cut off, so it's pitch black. The entrance to the bunker is blocked by a heavily reinforced steel vault door. Once that is opened, the path to the blueprint is filled with a crisscrossing laser security grid. We need an agent in the spy van reading the maps to guide the team over the radio."

**The Solution (4 Agents):**
- **Finn:** Navigates the pitch-black tunnels (night-vision) to lead the way. Avery cannot do this (afraid of dark).
- **Drew:** Uses demolitions to blow the reinforced vault door at the entrance.
- **Eden:** Contorts and flips through the inner laser security grid (gymnast).
- **Gwen:** Stays in the van as the communications tactician to guide the team.

### Mission Briefing 2: Operation Gala
**The Scenario:** "There is an exclusive French gala happening at a guarded mansion tonight. We need to steal a hard drive from the third-story penthouse. The courtyard is patrolled by fierce guard dogs, meaning the main entrance is blocked. We will need an agent to create a distraction at the front gate in the native language to buy us time. Someone else needs to bypass the courtyard, scale the outside of the building to the penthouse, and connect a wire so our tech expert can remotely crack the computer password from a safe distance."

**The Solution (4 Agents):**
- **Casey:** Creates the distraction at the gate (speaks French).
- **Hunter:** Tames the guard dogs in the courtyard so other agents could can pass.
- **Avery:** Scales the outside of the building to the 3rd-story balcony. Gwen cannot do this (afraid of heights).
- **Blake:** Stays off-site to remotely hack the computer password.

### Mission Briefing 3: Operation Gridlock
**The Scenario:** "Recruits, a highly sensitive data file is stored on a server protected by a pressure-sensitive floor grid. One wrong step, and the floor triggers a silent alarm that relocates the data. We need an incredibly precise, agile agent to cross the grid. They will be guided step-by-step by a second agent reading a digital map, who must be hardwired into the building's mainframe right outside the vault. The vault's security cycle is accelerating rapidly! We need an agent off-site in the van to remotely hack the network and slow down the cycle, while a fourth agent uses heavy tools in the electrical closet to physically cut the secondary hardwire relays."

**The Solution (4 Agents):**
- **Eden or Avery:** Crosses the pressure-sensitive grid (gymnast/highly precise). Hunter cannot do this (clumsy), and Drew cannot do this (his heavy 50lb backpack would trigger the pressure plates).
- **Gwen:** Hardwires into the mainframe to read the map and guide the team. Eden cannot do this (can't read maps).
- **Blake:** Acts as the remote hacker from the safety of the van. He cannot be on-site (cannot do physical tasks).
- **Drew:** Uses his gadgets and tools to handle the heavy hardware and cut the relays in the electrical closet.

### Mission Briefing 4: Operation Midnight Express
**The Scenario:** "A moving cargo train is transporting stolen tech through the mountains at night. The train will pass over a high bridge, which is the only place we can intercept it. We need an agent to drop onto the roof of the train from a helicopter above. Once on board, they must sneak past the armed guards in total darkness because the train cars have no lights. Meanwhile, another agent must override the train's computer system to stop the engine."

**The Solution (3 Agents):**
- **Avery:** Drops from the helicopter and lands safely on the train (rock climber/strong). Gwen cannot do this (afraid of heights).
- **Finn:** Navigates the pitch-black train cars to sneak past the guards (night-vision). Avery cannot do this (afraid of the dark).
- **Blake:** Overrides the train's computer system.

### Mission Briefing 5: Operation Zoo Break
**The Scenario:** "A rogue syndicate is hiding a weapons cache inside the animal enclosures at the city zoo. The cache is buried under an old concrete bunker in the center of the lion habitat. We need an agent to sneak in undetected, but the security cameras sweep the area every 30 seconds. Another agent needs to keep the lions calm and distracted so the team can enter safely. A third agent must plant explosives to blow open the concrete bunker. Once it's blown, an agent on the outside needs to read the complex zoo map and give everyone the fastest escape route over the radio."

**The Solution (4 Agents):**
- **Finn:** Sneaks past the security cameras undetected (stealth specialist). Hunter cannot do this (clumsy stealth) and Drew cannot do this (noisy).
- **Hunter:** Tames the lions to keep the team safe (animal wrangler). Casey cannot do this (allergic to dogs/animals).
- **Drew:** Plants explosives to blow open the concrete bunker (demolitions expert).
- **Gwen:** Reads the zoo map from outside and coordinates the escape route. Eden cannot do this (cannot read maps).

## Instructions
1. **Briefing:** The leader (acting as the "Intel Officer") welcomes the recruits. "Recruits, we have complex missions tonight, and 8 agents ready to go. But if we send the wrong team, or an agent whose weakness ruins the plan, the mission fails!"
2. **Review Agents:** Hand out the Agent Dossier cards to the kids. Have them read the skills and weaknesses out loud to the group.
3. **The Planning Phase:** The leader reads a Mission Briefing. The kids collaborate to select the exact right team of agents for the mission. 
   - *Younger kids (Ages 4-8):* Break the briefing down into obvious steps: "Who likes the dark? Who can do gymnastics?" Pin the chosen agents directly to the board.
   - *Older kids (Ages 9-12):* Give them the full scenario at once. Have them debate which agents to use.
4. **Debrief:** Once the team is selected, review the plan to make sure there are no errors.

### Key Talking Points
- **Spiritual Tie-in:** Just like the IMF needs different agents with different skills to complete a mission, God created each of us with unique gifts and talents. 
- **Scripture Connection:** Romans 12:4-6 or 1 Corinthians 12. "Just as a body, though one, has many parts, but all its many parts do not have the same function... we have different gifts."
- **Application:** We shouldn't be jealous of someone else's "mission" or talents. When we all do the part God planned for us, we accomplish His mission together as the Church. God's plan only works when everyone plays their specific role!
