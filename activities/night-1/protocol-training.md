# Protocol Training: "Agent Says"

**Station Type:** Physical/Game
**Location:** Open space in a room or outside

## Overview
A high-stakes variant of the classic "Simon Says" game. The leader acts as a strict IMF "Training Instructor." Kids must follow rapid-fire physical instructions, but *only* if preceded by the phrase "Agent says." 

## Setup & Instructions
1. Gather the kids in an open area with enough space to spread out their arms without hitting each other.
2. The leader introduces themselves as the IMF Training Instructor. Explain that fieldwork is dangerous and requires absolute obedience to protocol. 
3. Explain the rules: kids must perform the action quickly, but ONLY if the leader says "Agent says" first. 
4. If a kid does the action without the command "Agent says," or if they do the wrong action, they trigger a "security alarm."
5. **Penalty adjustment for VBS:** Instead of sitting out (which leads to boredom and distraction), kids who mess up must step out of formation, perform 5 jumping jacks (to "reset their alarm"), and then they can rejoin the game.

## Command List

Mix these specialized agent commands with simple, classic commands to keep the kids guessing. Gradually increase the speed of the commands to raise the difficulty.

### Thematic Spy Commands (Complicated)
*Ensure you demonstrate these to the kids before the game starts so they know exactly what to do!*

*   **"Take Cover!"** – Kids must immediately drop into a crouching position on the floor and cover the back of their heads with their hands.
*   **"Scan the Perimeter!"** – Kids stand up straight, place one hand over their eyes (like a salute or shading from the sun), and slowly spin around in a complete circle.
*   **"Check Comms!"** – Kids put one finger to their ear, nod affirmatively, and say loudly, "Copy that!"
*   **"Evacuate!"** – Kids must run in place as fast as they can.
*   **"Freeze Frame!"** – Kids must freeze completely like statues. Any movement triggers the alarm.
*   **"Deploy Laser Grid!"** – Kids must pick one leg up and balance on the other, as if carefully stepping over an invisible laser tripwire. 
*   **"Hacker Mode!"** – Kids pretend to type furiously on an imaginary keyboard in the air.

### Classic Commands (Simple)
*Use these to lull them into a rhythm before snapping back to the thematic commands.*

*   Touch your nose.
*   Stand on one foot.
*   Reach for the sky.
*   Touch your toes.
*   Pat your head and rub your tummy.
*   Do a silly dance.
*   Spin around once.
*   Hop like a frog.

## Age Adjustments
*   **Younger Kids (Ages 4-8):** Keep the pace moderate. Stick to mostly classic commands or the simplest thematic ones ("Take Cover", "Freeze Frame"). Emphasize the fun of messing up and doing jumping jacks over being strict.
*   **Older Kids (Ages 9-12):** Be very strict as the Instructor. Talk incredibly fast. Try to trick them by doing an action yourself that doesn't match the command you just shouted (e.g., saying "Agent says touch your nose" while you touch your ear).

## Key Talking Points (Spiritual Tie-In)
*   **The Lesson:** Emphasize the absolute necessity of following instructions exactly. When we go rogue and try our own way in the field, we fail the mission and put others in danger.
*   **The Connection:** God gives us instructions in the Bible because He loves us and sees the dangers that we can't see. Just like an IMF agent trusts their commander, we are called to trust and obey God's commands.
