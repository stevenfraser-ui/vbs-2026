## Relevance to Investigation

PINNACLE DATA submitted a **competing bid** to house The Light when the government sought private-sector hosting solutions. Their proposal was ultimately **rejected**.

## Bid Details

PINNACLE DATA's proposal included:
- Hosting at their largest facility: **Site SUMMIT** (capacity: 30 MW)
- Guaranteed 99.99% uptime
- Physical security including armed guards and biometric access
- Proposed cost: $[REDACTED] per year

## Reason for Rejection

The government evaluation committee rejected PINNACLE's bid citing:

> "Insufficient power infrastructure and cooling capacity. PINNACLE DATA's largest facility (30 MW) cannot accommodate the subject's current requirements, let alone its projected growth trajectory. The subject's power demands exceed anything in PINNACLE's entire portfolio by **an order of magnitude**. Additionally, PINNACLE lacks the purpose-built containment infrastructure this project demands."

## Assessment

PINNACLE DATA is a legitimate technology company, but they operate at a fundamentally different scale than what The Light requires. Their rejection confirms that The Light's hosting requirements are extraordinary — beyond what even a $12 billion data center company can provide.

**Note:** Some intercepted communications mention PINNACLE DATA in connection with The Light. These references are to the **rejected bid** only. The Light was never housed at any PINNACLE facility. Investigators should not waste resources pursuing the PINNACLE lead.
