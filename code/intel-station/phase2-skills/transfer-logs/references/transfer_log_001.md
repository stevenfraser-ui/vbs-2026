## Subject

Transfer of software asset designated **"The Light"** from origin facility to secondary hosting site.

## Origin Facility

**Lab Zero** — the personal laboratory and workspace of the asset's creator (see code name: LOGOS). A modest facility consisting of a single high-performance server rack connected to a residential-grade power supply.

- **Location:** [REDACTED]
- **Power Capacity:** 0.5 MW
- **Computing Resources:** 1 server rack, 4 GPUs, standard cooling

## Reason for Transfer

Within **weeks** of initial deployment, The Light's computational requirements exceeded Lab Zero's total capacity. System logs show:

- CPU utilization: sustained 100% across all cores
- Memory usage: exceeded available RAM within 72 hours of activation
- Power draw: tripped facility circuit breakers 3 times in one week
- Cooling system failure: ambient temperature reached critical levels

LOGOS personally requested emergency relocation, stating:

> "It's growing faster than I ever imagined. This room can't hold it anymore. We need real infrastructure — university-grade at minimum."

## Transfer Destination

[REDACTED] — University Computing Center. Details in separate facility assessment (see FR-217).

## Transfer Method

The Light was migrated via encrypted data transfer over a dedicated fiber-optic line. Transfer took 14 hours due to the sheer volume of data. No physical media was transported.

## Notes

This is the **first recorded relocation** of The Light. LOGOS accompanied the transfer personally and supervised installation at the destination facility.
