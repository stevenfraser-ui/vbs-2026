## SOURCE STATEMENT

*The following is a direct transcript of the informant's statement, lightly edited for clarity:*

---

"I've been installing industrial flooring systems for fifteen years. Aerospace clean rooms, pharmaceutical labs, military facilities — I've seen it all. But the job for Frost Veil... that was different.

First of all, Frost Veil itself was strange. Brand new company, two directors I'd never heard of, and suddenly they've got an $18 million contract for a single corridor? The money was insane for the scope of work.

They flew us to **Svalbard** — yes, the Arctic. Underground facility, three levels down. We were escorted everywhere. Couldn't bring phones. Couldn't talk to anyone outside our team. They had us sign NDAs with penalty clauses I'd never seen before.

The floor panels themselves — military-grade pressure sensors embedded in every single tile. **64 tiles in an 8-by-8 grid.** Each one calibrated to detect anything heavier than 2 kilograms. That's less than a bag of flour. You couldn't drop a book on this floor without it registering.

The panels look identical to normal flooring. You'd walk into that corridor and have no idea you were stepping on a sensor grid. That's the point.

Here's the thing that really got me: our system was designed to receive instructions from some other system about which tiles to 'arm' and which to leave 'safe.' But **we never saw that other system.** They told us another team would handle it. We installed the hardware, tested it, and left. We have no idea how they decide which tiles are dangerous and which aren't.

Whatever they're protecting down there, they are **terrified** of someone walking across that floor."
