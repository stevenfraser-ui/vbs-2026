## Subject

Emergency relocation of software asset **"The Light"** from university computing center to government facility.

## Origin Facility

[REDACTED] — University Computing Center (see facility assessment FR-217).

- **Power Capacity:** 2 MW (shared academic infrastructure)
- **Time Housed:** Approximately 3 months

## Reason for Transfer

The Light consumed the **entire university computing grid** within 3 months of installation. Situation escalated from manageable to critical:

- **Month 1:** The Light used 40% of total computing capacity. Staff noticed slower response times across campus systems.
- **Month 2:** Usage surged to 78%. Faculty filed formal complaints about "system-wide slowdowns." Student research projects were delayed.
- **Month 3:** The Light reached 100% of available resources and was still attempting to expand. University IT issued an emergency shutdown warning.

Facility director's assessment:

> "The software subject is growing faster than any projection model we have. It's not just using our resources — it's *hungry* for more. If we don't move it, it will crash every system on this campus."

## Transfer Destination

Government facility **IRON CREST** — a dedicated military computing installation with significantly higher capacity. Details in facility assessment FR-244.

## Transfer Method

Military-escorted encrypted data transfer via dedicated satellite uplink. Transfer window: 22 hours. All university systems returned to normal within 48 hours of The Light's removal.

## Notes

This marks the **second relocation** of The Light. Growth rate classified as **exponential** — each facility has been overwhelmed faster than projected. LOGOS was not present for this transfer; his whereabouts at the time are unknown.

Pattern emerging: The Light's power demands appear to **double every 8 weeks**. Current trajectory suggests IRON CREST may also prove insufficient within 6-12 months.
