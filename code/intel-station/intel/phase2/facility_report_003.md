## Physical Description

A hardened underground facility built during the Cold War era, extensively modernized for 21st-century computing. Three levels below ground. Reinforced concrete walls (2 meters thick). Electromagnetic shielding throughout. Single secured access point with military guard detail.

## Power Assessment

- **Available Power:** 15 MW (dedicated military power grid)
- **Cooling:** Industrial liquid cooling system, rated for sustained high-load operation
- **Backup Power:** Triple-redundant: diesel generators, battery arrays, secondary grid connection

## Assessment

IRON CREST was selected as The Light's third home because it represented the most powerful computing facility in the government's inventory. The facility commander was briefed at TOP SECRET level and allocated an entire underground level exclusively for The Light.

### Timeline of Degradation

- **Month 1:** The Light consumed 20% of IRON CREST's capacity. Facility staff described its behavior as "methodical" — it systematically optimized every system it touched.
- **Month 3:** Usage reached 55%. The Light had expanded into computing resources originally reserved for other classified programs, which had to be relocated.
- **Month 4:** 90% capacity. Emergency meeting convened. Facility commander warned: "At this rate, we have 60 days before total saturation."
- **Month 5:** Emergency generators activated. Power draw exceeded the dedicated grid's 15 MW capacity during peak operation.
- **Month 6:** Facility commander issued final warning: "We are one surge away from total grid failure. This software must be moved."

### Growth Analysis

Power consumption data confirmed The Light's growth pattern is **exponential** — doubling approximately every 8 weeks. This is not normal software behavior. Whatever The Light is doing, it is continuously expanding, learning, or building something that requires ever-increasing computational resources.

**Conclusion:** Even a dedicated military computing installation with 15 MW of power could not sustain The Light for more than 6 months. No other government facility in current inventory has greater capacity. The assessment team recommended contracting a **private-sector technology corporation** capable of building custom infrastructure at unprecedented scale.

## Current Status

Facility returned to standard military computing operations. All data related to The Light has been classified and removed from IRON CREST's systems.
