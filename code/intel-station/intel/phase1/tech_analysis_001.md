# TECHNICAL ANALYSIS — ENERGY SIGNATURE REPORT

## Objective

Analyze the energy requirements associated with The Light based on all available field data.

## Findings

Based on data collected from Operation MERIDIAN (server facility), satellite thermal scans (SUR-7741), and signal traffic analysis, we can determine the following:

### Power Requirements
- Any site housing The Light requires approximately **45-50 megawatts** of continuous power
- This is equivalent to the power consumption of a **small city** (roughly 30,000-40,000 homes)
- The power is used primarily for **computational processing and cooling systems**

### Cooling Requirements
- All confirmed sites show massive cooling infrastructure
- Thermal signatures indicate server-grade cooling systems, not weapons containment or energy shielding

### Infrastructure Pattern
- The Light requires **servers** — rows and rows of high-powered computing equipment
- Supporting infrastructure: backup power generators, redundant fiber optic networks, climate control

## Assessment

Whatever The Light is, protecting it and running it requires **massive computational power**. This energy profile is consistent with an extraordinarily large and complex piece of software — far more demanding than any known program. It is NOT consistent with storing a physical weapon, gemstone, or energy source.
