## Physical Description

A two-story building housing the university's central computing resources. Shared by approximately 200 faculty researchers and 3,000 students. Climate-controlled server room on the ground floor, offices on the second floor.

## Power Assessment

- **Available Power:** 2 MW (shared across entire facility)
- **Cooling:** Central HVAC with dedicated server room cooling
- **Backup Power:** Diesel generator (emergency only)

## Assessment

The Light was installed under the cover of a "classified government research project." University administrators were told only that a sensitive computing task would require dedicated resources for a "brief period."

### Timeline of Degradation

- **Week 1-2:** The Light consumed approximately 10% of total computing capacity. No noticeable impact on other users.
- **Month 1:** Usage climbed to 40%. Faculty reported slower processing times for research simulations.
- **Month 2:** Usage reached 78%. Formal complaints filed by 14 faculty members. Student projects experienced significant delays. IT department launched an investigation into "unusual system load."
- **Month 3:** The Light consumed 100% of available resources and was still attempting to expand. University IT issued an emergency shutdown warning, threatening to pull the plug entirely.

**Conclusion:** A 2 MW shared academic facility was never equipped to handle The Light's growth. The software outgrew this environment in approximately 90 days. Faculty described the experience as "something eating our computers from the inside."

## Current Status

Facility returned to normal academic operations after The Light's removal. All traces of the classified project have been scrubbed from university records.
